# Academic policies and procedures

## Academic integrity

Carleton's Academic Integrity Policy applies to all coursework, project
submissions, and theses. Violations include plagiarism, unauthorized
collaboration, falsification of data, and improper use of generative AI tools.
Confirmed violations are reported to the Dean of the Faculty of Graduate
Studies and may result in failure of the course, suspension, or expulsion.

The use of generative AI in coursework is governed by each instructor's
syllabus. When AI use is permitted, students must disclose how the tool was
used and retain a record of prompts and outputs.

## Time limits

The maximum time to complete the MEng is four years from initial registration
for full-time students, and seven years for part-time students. Extensions
beyond these limits require approval from the Faculty of Graduate Studies and
are granted only in exceptional circumstances.

## Leave of absence

Students may request a leave of absence of up to two consecutive terms for
medical, personal, or compassionate reasons. Leaves do not count toward the
program time limit but must be requested in writing before the start of the
term in which the leave begins.

## Withdrawal

Voluntary withdrawal from the program requires written notice to the MEng
coordinator. Refunds for tuition follow the schedule published by the
Registrar's Office. Withdrawal does not erase prior academic standing or any
outstanding fees.

## Convocation

Students who complete all program requirements are eligible to convocate at
the next ceremony following the term in which their final requirement is
satisfied. Spring convocation occurs in June; fall convocation occurs in
November.
