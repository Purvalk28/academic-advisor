# MEng Project (SERG 5908)

The MEng Project is the capstone of the Software Engineering Practice program.
It is a 0.5-credit course taken in the final term, and it integrates the
technical and methodological skills developed across the program into a single
substantive engineering work.

## Scope

The project must address a real software engineering problem of sufficient
complexity to demonstrate graduate-level mastery. Typical projects include:

- Designing and implementing a non-trivial software system
- Conducting an empirical study of a software engineering technique
- Building a tool that supports software development practice
- Performing a systematic comparison of competing approaches to a known
  software engineering problem

Pure literature reviews do not satisfy the project requirement.

## Supervision

Each project is supervised by a full-time faculty member from the School of
Computer Science, the Department of Systems and Computer Engineering, or the
Department of Electronics. Students are responsible for identifying a
supervisor and securing their agreement before registering for SERG 5908.

## Deliverables

Three deliverables are required:

1. A project proposal of approximately 8 to 10 pages, submitted within the
   first four weeks of the term
2. A final written report of approximately 30 to 50 pages, formatted according
   to the Faculty of Graduate Studies thesis preparation guidelines
3. An oral presentation and defense of approximately 45 minutes, attended by
   the supervisor and one additional faculty examiner

## Evaluation

The project is graded as satisfactory or unsatisfactory rather than receiving a
letter grade. An unsatisfactory result requires a revised submission within
three months. A second unsatisfactory result results in failure of the project
and withdrawal from the program.
