# Master of Engineering — Software Engineering Practice

The Master of Engineering in Software Engineering Practice (MEng SEP) is a
professional graduate program at Carleton University designed for students who
want to deepen their software engineering expertise without committing to
research-thesis work.

## Program structure

The MEng SEP requires a total of 5.0 credits. This breaks down as:

- 2.0 credits of core software engineering courses (SERG 5101, SERG 5102,
  SERG 5103, and SERG 5104)
- 2.5 credits of technical electives chosen from approved graduate course lists
  in COMP, SYSC, and ELEC
- 0.5 credits for the MEng Project (SERG 5908), which serves as a capstone

Students complete the program in approximately 16 to 24 months on a full-time
basis. Part-time options are available with permission of the department.

## Co-op pathway

Students enrolled in the MEng SEP co-op stream complete one or two industry
work terms in addition to the academic requirements. Each work term counts as
SERG 5500 (first term) or SERG 5501 (second term) and is graded pass/fail. The
co-op stream typically extends total program length by one to two terms.

## Admission requirements

Applicants need an undergraduate degree in computer science, software
engineering, electrical engineering, or a closely related field. A minimum
B+ average (76 percent) in the final two years of undergraduate study is
required. International applicants must demonstrate English proficiency through
TOEFL or IELTS at the levels published by the Faculty of Graduate Studies.
